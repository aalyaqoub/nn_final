Forgetting or Supressing For DS 4440

An Analysis of Erasing Concepts from Diffusion Models

Paper Overview

Diffusion models are trained on vast amounts of random images from the internets, and as a result, they produce undesirable things like styles of nonconsenting artists or NSFW content. Previous methods for removing concepts from models were either too expensive or easy to circumvent. Training on cleaned datasets requires costly retraining of models. And post generation filtering, similar to the one deployed by Stable Diffusion, are easy to bypass by novice tinkerers.

This paper introduces a new method to finetune models such that undesirable concepts can be removed from the model post training. The model achieves this by introducing a new constraint with the goal of minimizing outputs of a particular class or concept. There are two ways to apply these new constraints, on cross attention layers or non-cross attention layers. They can be applied at cross attention layers to remove targeted concepts like artists styles or non-cross attention layers to erase global concepts like cars.

Testing Points

  • Can you relearn something? If so, it would still not be safe to release the weights. Is the relearn concept of the same quality compared with the quality of pre-fine tuning.
  • Does this method have different effects on different model sizes? Are the ramifications of “forgetting” more severe for smaller models?
  • Is the model “unlearning” concepts or is it simply suppressing knowledge from being expressed? - Can this be tested by how easy it is for it to relearn something vs learn something new? (How can we test its learning new things)
  • If we use images that the model is not trained on, is the erasure still good? Benchmark if erasure is reliant on generalization.
